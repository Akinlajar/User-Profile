MY BEST FOOD

Rice and Stew and beans is a popular and versatile dish enjoyed in many parts of the world, especially in Africa. It consists of plain boiled rice served with a flavorful tomato-based stew, often enriched with spices, vegetables, and proteins such as chicken, beef, or fish. The combination of tender rice and richly seasoned stew makes it a comfort food loved by many.

  1. 2 cups of rice
  2. 6 large tomatoes (or a can of tomato paste)
  3. 1 onion (chopped)
  4. 2-3 cloves of garlic (minced)
  5. 1-2 bell peppers (optional)
  6. 1-2 chili peppers (for spice)
  7. 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  8. 1 teaspoon of curry powder
  9. 1 teaspoon of thyme
  10. Salt and pepper to taste
  11. Protein (chicken, beef, or fish, as desired)

Steps to Cook Rice:

  1. Rinse the rice: Rinse the rice thoroughly under cold water to remove excess starch.
  2. Boil the rice: Bring a pot of water to a boil, add a pinch of salt, and then add the rice. Cook for about 15-20 minutes until tender. Drain the water and set the rice aside.

THE PLACE I WOULD LOVE TO VISIT

I would love to visit Norway someday, Norway is a must-visit destination for anyone seeking a blend of natural beauty, rich culture, and unique experiences. Famous for its breathtaking landscapes, Norway is home to dramatic fjords, snow-capped mountains, and stunning coastal scenery. Whether you're hiking in the pristine wilderness or taking a scenic boat cruise through the fjords, Norway offers an unparalleled connection to nature.

One of the main draws of Norway is the chance to witness the Northern Lights. This natural phenomenon, best seen from the northern regions like Tromsø, offers a magical display of dancing lights in the sky that leaves visitors in awe. If you're visiting in the winter, Norway’s snowy landscapes are perfect for skiing, snowboarding, and dog sledding, making it a winter wonderland for adventure seekers.

Norway is also a haven for culture enthusiasts. Its capital, Oslo, is a vibrant city filled with world-class museums, galleries, and modern architecture. You can explore the rich Viking history at the Viking Ship Museum or visit the renowned Munch Museum to see "The Scream" up close. The country’s commitment to sustainability and design is evident throughout its cities, offering a harmonious blend of the old and the new.

Beyond the urban attractions, Norway’s villages and small towns offer a glimpse into a slower, more traditional way of life. Places like Bergen, with its colorful wooden houses and proximity to fjords, provide a charming contrast to modern city life. The Lofoten Islands, with their picturesque fishing villages and rugged coastline, are perfect for travelers seeking tranquility and stunning views.

Food lovers will enjoy the fresh, local seafood and traditional Norwegian dishes like rakfisk (fermented fish) and klippfisk (dried and salted fish). If you're visiting during the summer, Norway’s long daylight hours—referred to as the Midnight Sun—provide a unique opportunity to explore the country’s beauty under continuous daylight.

Whether you’re drawn by the northern lights, the fjords, or the rich culture, Norway offers an unforgettable experience for every traveler. Its combination of natural wonders, adventure, and cultural richness makes it a top destination for anyone seeking to explore the best of the great outdoors and Scandinavian life.
